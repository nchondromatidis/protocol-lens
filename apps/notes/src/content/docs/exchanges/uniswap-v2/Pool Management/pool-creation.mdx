The theory behind a Uniswap pool, also known as a pair, is rooted in the concept of an **Automated Market Maker (AMM)**. Below is a detailed breakdown of how these protocols function, with a focus on the constant product model.

## Fundamental Concepts

### Automated Market Makers (AMMs)

AMMs are the underlying protocols used by decentralized exchanges (DEXs) to enable **permissionless, middleman-free trading**.

#### Replacing Order Books

Traditional order books require intermediaries to match "coincidences of wants" and are economically inefficient on-chain due to the **high gas costs** of creating or canceling individual limit orders.

#### Mathematical Pricing

In an AMM, prices are not determined by a central entity but by **mathematical formulae** written into the open-source code of the protocol.

#### Instant Liquidity

AMMs provide **instant feedback on rates and slippage**, allowing for immediate execution regardless of whether a specific buyer or seller is present at that moment.

### The Constant Product Market Maker (CPMM)

The specific type of AMM used by Uniswap v2 is the **Constant Product Market Maker**, which is governed by the formula

<p className="text-center">$x \cdot y = k$ </p>

#### The Invariant ($k$)

In this formula, $x$ and $y$ represent the reserve balances of the two tokens in the pool, and $k$ is the **invariant**. The protocol requires that $k$ remains constant during a trade (ignoring fees).

#### The Price Mechanism

The **spot price** is determined by the ratio of the tokens in the pool

<p className="text-center"> $p = y/x$ </p>

When a trader buys (withdraws) token $x$, they must deposit a proportional amount of token $y$ to ensure the product $x \cdot y$ remains equal to $k$.

#### Arbitrage and Market Alignment

The pool price follows global market rates because **external arbitrageurs** monitor price discrepancies.

If the pool price is "incorrect" compared to the market, arbitrageurs will trade against the pool until the internal ratio matches the global rate, earning a profit while realigning the pool's price.

## Uniswap V2 concepts

### The Uniswap V2 a Liquidity Pool (Pair)

A Uniswap liquidity pool is a **smart contract that holds balances of two unique ERC-20 tokens** and enforces rules for swapping, depositing, and withdrawing them.

### Arbitrary Pairs

Uniswap v2 allows for the creation of liquidity pools for any two arbitrary ERC-20 tokens, removing the requirement from v1 that every pair includes ETH.

### WETH Requirement

Because the core codebase is designed for arbitrary ERC-20 pairs, native ETH is not supported; it must be wrapped into WETH to interact with the protocol

### Deterministic Addressing

Pair contracts are instantiated by a single **factory contract**.

It uses the Ethereum `CREATE2` opcode to generate a deterministic address for the pair contract based on the addresses of the two tokens, allowing the address to be calculated off-chain without querying the blockchain state.
